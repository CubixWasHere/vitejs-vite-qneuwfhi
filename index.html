Starting Grid

Before every Formula 1 race, drivers qualify to determine their starting positions. Pole Position is awarded to the fastest driver in qualifying.

Pole Position

The fastest qualifying lap starts from P1.

Formation Lap

Drivers complete one lap before the race officially starts.

Five Red Lights

When all five lights go out, the Grand Prix begins.

Grid Penalties

Drivers can receive penalties that move them backwards.

Pit Crew

A Formula 1 pit stop is one of the fastest team operations in the world. Every mechanic has one important job.

Front Jack

Lifts the front of the car before the tyres are changed.

Rear Jack

Raises the rear of the car and lowers it after the stop.

Tyre Gun Operators

Remove and tighten the wheel nuts using high-speed pneumatic guns.

Tyre Changers

Remove old tyres and fit a fresh set.

Wing Mechanics

Replace damaged front wings if required.

Release Controller

Signals when the driver can leave the pit box safely.

Aerodynamics

Aerodynamics controls how air moves around the car, producing downforce while reducing drag.

Front Wing

Controls airflow around the front tyres and generates front downforce.

Floor

Creates ground effect which generates most of the car's grip.

Sidepods

Guide airflow while cooling the power unit.

Rear Wing

Creates rear downforce and houses the DRS flap.

Formula 1 Rules

Formula 1 follows strict sporting and technical regulations to ensure fair, safe and competitive racing.

Yellow Flag

Slow down. Overtaking is not allowed.

Red Flag

The race is stopped due to dangerous conditions.

Blue Flag

A slower driver must allow a faster car to pass.

Safety Car

The field slows down while marshals clear incidents.

Track Limits

Drivers must stay within the white lines.

Penalties

Drivers can receive time or grid penalties for breaking rules.
